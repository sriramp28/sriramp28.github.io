About

Built Around Hard Physics.

I am a thermofluids and transport-physics researcher, computational simulation specialist, and educator trained across mechanical engineering, aerospace engineering, applied mathematics, and high-performance scientific computing. My work centers on physical systems where flow, heat, scalar mixing, chemistry, acoustics, structure, and scale interactions must be understood together rather than as isolated effects.

I build and analyze numerical models, run large simulations, develop postprocessing algorithms, and turn dense fields into interpretable physical arguments. I am especially interested in work where rigorous computation informs scientific interpretation, model assessment, finite-element and coupled-field analysis, thermal-fluid behaviour, environmental transport, acoustic response, and practical technical decisions.

I have been involved in the design and optimization of several jet engine configurations subject to stringent emissions, noise, and stability requirements. That work required a complex interplay of flow physics, structural mechanics, thermal loading, acoustic response, manufacturability, and practical engineering design, and it shaped the way I approach simulation as an iterative design tool rather than as a post hoc diagnostic. My applied work has included fuel-injector assessment, droplet-cloud evaporation and heat-transfer analysis, wall-cooling and air-bleed evaluation, combustor flow-rate and geometry optimization, duct acoustics, and coupled CFD/FEA interpretation of engine structures.

I also teach aerodynamics, computational fluid dynamics, fluid mechanics, and computing with the same emphasis on physical interpretation. Whether the topic is lift generation, finite-difference stability, odd-even decoupling in the heat equation, or simulation workflow, I try to help students see both the mathematics and the physical consequence of a model.

I am open to opportunities where advanced simulation, transport physics, multiphysics analysis, and technical communication can support demanding scientific or applied programmes, especially when careful modeling has to translate into usable physical insight.

Research identity

From equations to interpretable physical insight.

My work combines governing-equation modeling, mesh-based discretization, algorithm development, high-performance computation, and diagnostics that convert large simulation outputs into physical arguments. I focus on explaining what mechanisms dominate, where models lose information, and which quantities remain reliable when a system is simplified. In applied settings, that same habit extends to finite-element modeling, thermo-structural response, vibration and acoustic constraints, CAD-informed geometry changes, manufacturing-aware tradeoffs, and iterative design decisions.

Scientific range

Transferable across natural and engineered systems.

The problems I work on involve transport, thermal gradients, instability, turbulence, acoustic response, reacting mixtures, filtering, structural response, and model assessment. The same numerical habits apply broadly across thermal systems, ocean mixing, environmental transport, propulsion, energy conversion, process flows, room acoustics, aeroacoustics, fluid-structure interaction, thermo-structural response, mechanical design, manufacturability studies, and coupled-field simulation. I am comfortable moving between first-principles modeling, CAD/mesh preparation, parametric simulation, postprocessing, and the practical constraints that enter when a modeled design has to be built, inspected, or operated.

Communication

From analysis to usable physical judgment.

I care about turning complex simulations into evidence that can guide scientific and technical judgment. That means nondimensionalization, careful visualization, quantitative comparison, reproducible workflows, and writing that connects diagnostics to physical interpretation, design tradeoffs, mechanical constraints, thermal margins, and model limitations. The same communication style carries into teaching, where numerical artifacts, governing equations, and computed fields have to become ideas students can reason with independently.

Service and life outside work

Campus service and creative practice.

At Georgia Tech, I serve on several advisory committees in different capacities, with work spanning student experience, graduate education, academic policy, and community-facing service. Outside the office, I am an avid archer, runner, musician, linguist, and poet.
