Engineering focus

Simulation for engineering design and physical insight.

The applications of my research span aerospace, energy, propulsion, thermal management, process flows, and advanced manufacturing contexts where design decisions depend on flow, heat, acoustics, transport, and coupled multiphysics behavior.

01

Fluid flow and turbulence

Analysis of shear layers, jets, separated flows, coherent structures, transition, mixing, and turbulence organization using high-fidelity simulation, modal decompositions, spectra, wavelets, topology, and event-based diagnostics.

02

Heat transfer and reacting systems

Quantification of thermal transport, scalar mixing, chemical heat release, entropy production, and thermochemical coupling in flows where gradients and localized events dominate system behavior.

03

Acoustics and compressibility

Characterization of acoustic-compressive activity, wave-vortex interaction, broadband combustion noise, pressure-dilatation behavior, room and enclosure acoustics, wave propagation, and compressibility effects in high-speed and reacting flows.

04

Simulation workflows

Development of numerical workflows for model construction, mesh-based discretization, HPC execution, filtering, nondimensionalization, field export, quantitative diagnostics, visualization, and ParaView-ready inspection.

Applied simulation capability

Research outputs built from modeling, computation, and physical interpretation.

The core of my work combines governing-equation modeling, algorithm development, high-performance execution, model-form assessment, transport analysis, unsteady signal processing, numerical diagnostics, and clear communication of dense simulation results for design, analysis, and engineering education contexts.
