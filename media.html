Visuals and movies

Seeing the Physics.

Simulation visuals reveal structure that is difficult to see from scalar metrics alone: coherent motion, localized heat release, acoustic-compressive activity, transport barriers, and the geometry of mixing.

Sound in an absorptive room

The acoustic field expands from a speaker-like source, reflects from the room boundaries, and weakens near an absorptive wall. The companion error plot shows why high-order schemes are useful for room acoustics: they preserve wave speed and phase more accurately over time.

Heat-release dynamics in an unstable afterburner

The flame does not simply fluctuate in place. Heat release rolls, stretches, and reorganizes in time, giving a direct view of the unsteady combustion process that can feed acoustic instability.

Thermal evolution in a reacting mixing layer

The movie shows how hot and cold fluid regions stretch, fold, and reorganize in time, while the topology map connects that thermal evolution to vortical and reactive structure. Together they show why reacting turbulent mixing cannot be reduced to a single instantaneous field.

Turbulence-state geometry

The barycentric prism visualizes how local turbulence state, mixture fraction, and heat release occupy a coupled state space rather than varying independently.

Scale-local interaction

Transform-space views show which interaction families dominate across position and scale, making multiscale coupling visible as a structured map rather than a single integrated number.

Low-frequency organization

Phase maps reveal intermittent windows where heat release and kinematic activity organize together at low frequency, exposing collective behavior that is easy to miss in raw time traces.

Bluff-body velocity field

The wake behind the bluff body creates the residence-time and recirculation environment that anchors the flame. Those same flow features influence where incomplete combustion products can persist downstream.

Instability operating map

The map shows where operating conditions bring heat-release oscillations and acoustic response into a reinforcing relationship, which is the practical danger zone for combustion instability.

Shock-induced vorticity

As the shocks collide and reflect, the pressure waves leave behind rotational structure. The movie makes the wave-vorticity exchange visible without reducing it to a single scalar trace.

Jet-in-crossflow structure

The jet bends, rolls up, and mixes with the crossflow through coherent vortical structures. The visual links injection geometry to mixing, flame placement, and thermal loading.

Separated-flow wall structure

Separation creates a large unsteady region above the wall, while the near-wall vorticity shows how that motion imprints itself on the surface before reattachment.
