Research

Mechanisms Across Scales.

Physical systems rarely organise through a single scale. My research connects local transport, turbulent structure, acoustic response, scalar mixing, chemical activity, and numerical modeling to explain how complex behaviour emerges in natural and engineered thermofluid systems.

Mechanisms across scales

Ocean mixing, reacting flows, acoustics, filtering, topology, and transport.

My research connects application-scale behavior with the smaller-scale mechanisms that control transport, instability, sound generation, scalar interfaces, ocean mixing, and numerical representation.

Ocean mixing and double diffusion

Salt-water fingering in a finite-depth Boussinesq layer

Current salt-fingering work examines how warm, salty fluid above colder, fresher fluid develops double-diffusive instability when heat diffuses more rapidly than salt. The simulation resolves the onset and slow nonlinear growth of salinity, temperature, and vertical-velocity structure in a three-dimensional finite-depth Boussinesq setting, with an emphasis on flux pathways, interface deformation, and the transition from seeded perturbations toward organised finger-like transport.

Related preprints: arXiv:2606.21131 and arXiv:2606.26045.

Applied propulsion design

Simulation-guided design of engine configurations under emissions, noise, and stability constraints

Applied propulsion work has connected injector design, droplet-cloud transport, evaporation and heat transfer, wall cooling, air-bleed profiles, combustor acoustics, emissions trends, and engine-geometry changes. These studies combine high-fidelity flow simulation with finite-element and coupled-field interpretation of structural response, so design changes can be judged against thermal loading, acoustic behavior, structural integrity, manufacturability, and regulatory constraints.

Combustion emissions

Emission structure in a bluff-body combustor

Practical combustors must balance stability, mixing, temperature rise, and emissions. These simulations connect the velocity field, downstream carbon-monoxide distribution, and flow-variable correlations to identify which regions and mechanisms most strongly influence pollutant formation. The same analysis framework supports combustor geometry and flow-rate trade studies where emissions reduction has to be balanced against thermal loading and operability.

Afterburner instability

Operating-state feedback in a practical afterburner

Combustion instability emerges when unsteady heat release and acoustic modes reinforce each other. The operating map links flow rate, heat-release fluctuation strength, and dominant acoustic frequency to identify feedback-prone regions, while the pressure spectrum highlights the loudest unstable modes. This work connects noise and stability requirements to combustor flow and structural design decisions.

Spray and propulsion systems

Droplet transport in an annular engine inlet

Liquid fuel placement and carrier-flow structure control evaporation, mixing, emissions, and operability. The visualizations show droplet distribution and the surrounding flow field in a supersonic engine-relevant configuration, highlighting how inlet nonuniformity can propagate into downstream combustion behavior. The design context includes injector assessment, droplet-cloud evolution, evaporation and heat-transfer rates, and downstream thermal-load implications.

Shock interaction

Vorticity generation in a four-shock Riemann problem

Colliding shock systems generate vorticity, pressure-wave interactions, and rapidly changing interface geometry. The movie visualizes vorticity induced by moving shocks with pressure contours overlaid, making the coupling between wave motion and rotational structure visible in time. The calculation is useful as a compact stress test for shock-capturing behavior, interface deformation, and the conversion of compressive motion into rotational flow.

Jet-in-crossflow combustion

Mixing and heat release in a methane-air jet-in-crossflow

Jet-in-crossflow injection is central to many air-breathing combustion systems. Vortical structures, streamlines, temperature projection, mixture fraction, and heat-release contours show how jet penetration, shear-layer rollup, and scalar mixing shape flame anchoring and thermal loading.

Separated-flow dynamics

Unsteady separation and reattachment over periodic hills

Separating and reattaching flows are common in diffusers, ducts, turbomachinery passages, and thermal management hardware. Isovolumes and wall-projected structures show how large coherent motion interacts with near-wall vorticity and reattachment dynamics.

Room acoustics

Sound propagation in an absorptive enclosure

A damped wave-equation simulation shows how sound emitted from a speaker-like source reflects, spreads, and decays inside an enclosed space with one absorbing wall. High-order schemes reduce numerical dispersion, which is essential when the goal is to predict how sound feels across a room rather than only whether a wave reaches a point.

Numerical simulation

Mesh-based modeling, discretization, and high-performance simulation workflows

Across my research, the technical workflow combines governing-equation modeling, mesh-based discretization, numerical reasoning shared across finite-volume and finite-element formulations, parallel execution, field reconstruction, and quantitative postprocessing for coupled transport, acoustic, and reacting-flow systems. It also includes practical geometry preparation, meshing, design sweeps, and interpretation across CFD, FEA, and coupled multiphysics tools. The emphasis is on building simulations that can be interrogated after the fact, so that fields, derived quantities, event sets, spectra, and reduced diagnostics remain traceable back to the underlying conservation laws.

Flow physics atlas

Topology, mixing, compressibility, and reaction physics in a supersonic hydrogen-air temporal mixing layer

A broad characterization of a reacting shear layer showing how vortical motion, scalar interfaces, heat release, baroclinicity, compressible work, and turbulence anisotropy occupy related but distinct supports. This study provides the physical baseline for the filtering and interscale analyses. The emphasis is on locating where momentum transport, mixing, thermal activity, and chemical response coincide, and where they separate into different regions of the layer.

Temporal dynamics

Temporal organization of heat release and kinematic activity in a turbulent mixing layer

Time-series diagnostics quantify recurrence, spectra, nonlinear dependence, and modal structure in heat release, dilatation, and vorticity signals extracted from the reacting layer. The resulting picture connects intermittent thermochemical bursts with the temporal organization of compressive and vortical activity, rather than treating the signals as isolated scalar histories.

Filtering time signatures

Box-filtering effects on time-series diagnostics of a reacting temporal mixing layer

Box filtering is treated as a diagnostic intervention on unsteady dynamics, revealing which amplitudes, timing signatures, phase relations, and coherent structures survive scale-selective attenuation. This branch connects directly to scale-resolved modeling questions while keeping the emphasis on physical behavior rather than one method family. The recurrence structure is especially useful because it shows how filtering changes the geometry of repeated states, not only the signal amplitude.

Low-frequency organization

Intermittent collective low-frequency organization in a turbulent reacting mixing layer

Phase-alignment maps and event composites identify intermittent windows where heat release, dilatation, and vortical activity become collectively organized at low frequency. These windows help separate persistent low-frequency coordination from short-lived amplitude bursts, giving a clearer view of when the reacting layer behaves as a coupled thermo-kinematic system.

Disturbance energetics

Modal energetics and disturbance-energy partition in a reacting shear layer

Acoustic, vortical, entropic, and cross-energy components are separated to clarify how a reacting shear layer stores disturbance energy across thermodynamic and kinematic branches. The decomposition makes it possible to ask whether energetic structure is primarily compressive, vortical, thermal, or mixed, which is difficult to infer from primitive variables alone.

Interaction budgets

Interaction budgets and multiscale coupling in a reacting shear layer

Mass-like, force-like, and heat-like interaction families are compared in physical space, PDFs, spectra, wavelets, and dominance maps to expose non-additive multiscale coupling. The comparison is designed to show whether scale-to-scale organization is carried by one dominant mechanism or by competing pathways that occupy different regions of transform space.

Filtered modal interactions

Box-filter robustness of modal interaction diagnostics in a reacting shear layer

Modal interaction diagnostics are filtered directly and through commutation checks to determine which coupling measures are robust enough for scale-resolved interpretation. The emphasis is on what reduced-resolution simulation can preserve, distort, or require modeling. This gives a direct bridge between modal physics and practical filtering decisions in reduced-resolution reacting-flow calculations.

Entropy and irreversibility

Filtering effects on entropy transport and entropy-production structure in a supersonic reacting shear layer

Entropy-like transport and production proxies show how filtering reshapes localized irreversible activity near sharp scalar, thermal, and compressive gradients. The analysis connects numerical scale removal with thermodynamic interpretation, highlighting where smoothing alters the apparent support of dissipation-like and entropy-producing processes.

Submitted to J. Fluid Mech. Rapids; preprint available as arXiv:2606.15399.

Interface geometry

Fractal geometry and multiscale connectivity of a reacting temporal mixing-layer interface

Mixture-fraction interfaces are analyzed through fractal dimension, connectivity, curvature, and thermochemical alignment to connect geometry with reaction-zone structure. The goal is to quantify how convoluted scalar surfaces organize heat release and mixing, rather than describing the interface only through visual complexity.

Lagrangian transport

Lagrangian coherent structures in a supersonic reacting temporal mixing layer

FTLE ridges locate attracting and repelling material structures that organize scalar transport, ridge intersections, and thermochemical signatures in the evolving layer. These structures provide a material view of mixing, showing how transport barriers and stretching regions guide where reactive scalars are brought together.

Submitted to Chaos; preprint available as arXiv:2606.20352.

Wave-vortex coupling

Local wave-vortex interaction in a reacting supersonic shear layer

Dilatation and vorticity diagnostics quantify local synchronization, lag, band-limited coupling, and convective-frame coactivity between acoustic-compressive and vortical fields. This separates genuine wave-vortex organization from simple spatial overlap, which is important in compressible shear layers where acoustic and hydrodynamic motions coexist.

Combustion noise

Near-field combustion-noise source dynamics in a reacting supersonic temporal mixing layer

Heat release, compression, geometry, and broadband acoustic signatures are linked to the feedback between reacting turbulence and sound generation. The analysis connects local reaction structure with time-frequency acoustic content, clarifying how broadband noise emerges from distributed, intermittent combustion activity.

Submitted to Combust. Theory Model.; preprint available as arXiv:2606.15626.

Reactive-acoustic packets

Reactive-acoustic wave packet dynamics in a supersonic reacting shear layer

Band-averaged dilatation isolates reactive-acoustic wave packets and quantifies their speeds, persistence, scales, and thermochemical environment. The group-velocity view turns localized compressive activity into measurable packet motion, making it possible to compare transport speed, lifetime, and reaction-zone association across frequency bands.

Receptivity

Receptivity and biorthogonal decomposition in a reacting temporal mixing layer

Direct and adjoint finite-thickness modes identify where localized forcing couples most strongly to Kelvin-Helmholtz receptivity in a chemically active shear layer. The biorthogonal formulation links spatial forcing, modal response, and shear-layer amplification, so the result identifies sensitive regions rather than only energetic structures.

Submitted to Theor. Comput. Fluid Dyn.; preprint available as arXiv:2606.20819.

Compression-chemistry coupling

Event-level compression-chemistry coupling in a supersonic reacting temporal mixing layer

Compression masks condition heat release and scalar-gradient activity to quantify overlap, proximity, lag, geometry, and event-scale coupling. The resulting diagnostics show whether compressive events merely pass through reacting regions or whether they preferentially align with chemically and scalar-active structures.

Submitted to Combust. Flame; preprint available as arXiv:2606.15628.
