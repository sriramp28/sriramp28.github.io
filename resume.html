Research Experience

High Performance Computing Lab, Georgia Tech

Research Assistant working on design, modeling, and simulation of reacting flows. Designed and analyzed a fuel injector for a supersonic commercial aviation engine; studied droplet-cloud behavior, evaporation, and heat-transfer rates; assessed wall-cooling mechanisms and air-bleed profiles for structural integrity; optimized flow rates and engine geometry to reduce emissions and thermal loads; and performed high-fidelity reacting-flow CFD coupled with FEA interpretation of engine structures.

2020-present

Ben T. Zinn Combustion Lab, Georgia Tech

Research Assistant studying combustion noise, flame-turbulence interaction, entropy waves, and thermoacoustic mechanisms. Used theoretical analysis, CFD, and FEA to assess combustion noise in a commercial jet-engine context and connect combustor flow and structural design choices to FAA-relevant noise constraints.

2019-2020

Computational Combustion Lab, Georgia Tech

Research Assistant on simulations of supercritical jets, reacting jet-in-crossflow, jet flames, separating-reattaching flows, and magnetohydrodynamic turbulence. Used simulations to evaluate ignition in high-pressure combustion engines, performed coupled CFD/FEA analysis of wall-flame interaction, developed a two-level-set model for pressure and stress profiles, and assessed hypersonic re-entry flow behavior using magnetohydrodynamic turbulence and coupled structural FEA.

2017-2019

Rarefied Gas Dynamics Lab, IIT Madras

Research Associate working on large-eddy simulations of screech in an afterburner and acoustics of a commercial boiler. Developed a multiscale model for high-frequency instability in a proprietary afterburner, including coupled acoustic-structural analysis, and used COMSOL to evaluate duct acoustics in upstream engine plumbing and its effects on fuel-air mixing, combustion efficiency, and structural integrity.

2013-2017
