Teaching profile

Rigor With Care.

My teaching is built around conceptual structure, active problem-solving, fair assessment, and practical engineering judgment. I want students to understand how equations become design tools, how assumptions shape predictions, and how numerical methods connect theory to real systems.

Teaching philosophy

Bottom-up structure, applied judgment.

I introduce ideas in their simplest form and progressively add physical effects, assumptions, and numerical tools. This approach helps students connect mathematics to physical meaning, develop intuition, and recognize where a model is useful, approximate, or incomplete.

Student support

High standards without unnecessary opacity.

My courses emphasize discussion-driven lectures, conceptual check-ins, live demonstrations, open office-hour support, and assessments that reward sustained understanding rather than isolated performance on a single high-stakes exam.

Odd-even decoupling in the heat equation.

This excerpt from AE 4040 introduces odd-even decoupling through a familiar parabolic model problem. The discussion connects the algebraic stencil, the physical expectation of diffusion, and the numerical artifact produced when a discretization supports nonphysical grid-scale modes.

Numerical methods in practice

From stencil behavior to engineering judgment.

The excerpt focuses on a simple but important warning in numerical work: satisfying an update formula is not the same as producing a physically sensible solution. Using the heat equation keeps the physics familiar, so the discussion can move naturally from the stencil to the unexpected grid-scale pattern and then to how students should recognize that kind of artifact in their own computations.

Classroom practice

Engineering education as modeling practice.

01

Conceptual scaffolding

Each course is organized around a clear progression from governing ideas to engineering use, so students see how assumptions and approximations enter the analysis.

02

Hands-on computation

Students implement numerical methods, debug simulations, visualize fields, and learn to connect code behavior with mathematical and physical constraints.

03

Project-based learning

Design-oriented projects replace rote repetition with judgment, tradeoff analysis, and open-ended engineering decisions wherever the course structure allows.

04

Fair assessment

Assignments, exams, optional challenges, and revision opportunities are structured to reward mastery, reflection, and improvement.
